# Support Policy

## Severity Levels

Sev-1 means a production system is down, data is unavailable, or a customer-facing workflow is blocked.

Sev-2 means the system is degraded but a workaround exists.

Sev-3 means a non-critical feature is broken or delayed.

Sev-4 means a question, enhancement request, or documentation issue.

## Escalation Rules

For Sev-1 incidents, the support engineer must page the on-call engineer immediately and open an incident bridge within 15 minutes.

For Sev-2 incidents, the support engineer must notify the product owner and create a tracking ticket within 2 business hours.

For Sev-3 incidents, the support engineer should create a normal backlog ticket.

For Sev-4 requests, the support engineer should route the request to documentation or product triage.

## Customer Communication

For Sev-1, send the first customer update within 30 minutes.

For Sev-2, send the first customer update within 4 business hours.

Never promise a root cause before engineering confirms it.
