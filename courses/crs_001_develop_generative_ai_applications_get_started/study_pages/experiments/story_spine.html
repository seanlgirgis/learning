The Help Desk Story

One team, one problem, one app — the same arc Coursera walks, told as a story instead of a concept chain. Existing field guides and cards stay as reference; this page is the through-line.

Cast: Sarah runs customer support. Thousands of product questions arrive daily. She does not need another classifier — she needs an assistant that drafts helpful replies. Your job in Course 1: build that assistant, one layer at a time.

Scene 1 · The wrong tool

Why generative AI — not just classification?

A spam filter (discriminative) labels mail. Sarah needs new draft text for each customer (generative). Foundation models and LLMs are the shared engine; prompting steers them for her desk.

Reference: Module 1 guide · Landscape map · RemNote 01

Scene 2 · Instructions that travel

Sarah writes prompts that scale

One-off strings break in production. PromptTemplate holds the mold; variables hold tonight’s product name. Strong prompts bundle instruction, context, input data, and output shape — so the desk stays consistent.

Scene 3 · Roles in the chat window

System, human, and the message list

The tutor behavior lives in system. The live customer question is human (API says “user” — same role). ChatPromptTemplate.from_messages builds the list; invoke fills it — then the model runs.

Lab 03 · RemNote 03 · RemNote 10

Scene 4 · The reusable pipeline

LCEL — prompt | model | parser

Sarah’s desk needs a repeatable pipeline, not a script spaghetti. The pipe connects components; chain.invoke() runs the whole path. Type coercion is automatic — she focuses on behavior, not glue code.

Lab 05 · Code patterns · LCEL bubble map · RemNote 04

Scene 5 · From prototype to product

Structured answers and delivery

Free text is not enough for ticketing systems — parsers and structured output produce reliable fields. Delivery might be an API or web route; evaluation asks “is Sarah’s team actually faster and accurate?”
